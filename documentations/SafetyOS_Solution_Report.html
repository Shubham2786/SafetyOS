01 Part I · Context

Executive Summary

SafetyOS is an AI-native intelligence layer for industrial operations. It fuses computer vision, OT/SCADA telemetry, IoT wearables, and enterprise workflows into a single explainable knowledge graph — turning latent signals into decisive foresight before an incident occurs.

Thesis

Industrial fatalities are not a data problem. They are a fusion, reasoning and cognitive-load problem. In every landmark incident of the last decade — from the Visakhapatnam Steel Plant coke-oven explosion of January 2025 to the refinery fires that preceded it — the signals existed. The intelligence layer did not.

SafetyOS closes that gap. It sits above existing SCADA, EHS and CCTV infrastructure as a decision-support surface, never as an actuator. Humans and Safety-Instrumented Systems remain in the loop. The platform's job is foresight, explanation and orchestration.

What we shipped

  • 27 modules · 466 features spanning edge perception, permits, LOTO, shift handover, incident RCA, emergency response, digital twin, and MLOps.
  • 48 microservices on a zero-trust API mesh with OIDC, SPIFFE mTLS and OPA-based ABAC.
  • Multi-agent reasoning layer with a Governance Meta-Agent enforcing an EU AI Act circuit-breaker (AG-020).
  • Halo design language — ISA-101 compliant, grayscale-first, WCAG 2.2 AAA on all safety-critical surfaces.

The Industrial Safety Landscape

Heavy industry runs on continuous shift operations and rigid departmental silos — Operations, Maintenance, HSE, Security. Each generates telemetry the others never see. The result is a persistent cognitive-load crisis at the frontline and a bureaucratic drag on the back office.

Structural forces

  • Data silos between IT (permits, HR), OT (SCADA, telemetry) and CV (cameras) mean no system holds the full picture.
  • Shift handovers are the single highest-risk moment for information loss in a continuous plant.
  • Contractor churn creates a permanent long tail of workers without site-specific hazard fluency.
  • Alarm floods — ISA-18.2 violations lead to fatigue and desensitization at the very console meant to prevent incidents.

The regulatory tightening

OSHA, OISD, PSM/RMP frameworks, and now the EU AI Act, are converging on a common expectation: not only must operations be safe, they must be demonstrably safe — with tamper-evident evidence chains, explainable AI decisions, and human oversight over any autonomous recommendation.

03 Part I · Context

Problem Statement

A modern refinery generates over one petabyte of operational data per year across CCTV, OT sensors, permits and mobile forms. Less than one percent is ever examined before an incident. The remaining 99% becomes evidence — after the fact.

“The signals existed. The intelligence layer did not.” — Root-cause finding, Visakhapatnam Steel Plant coke-oven explosion, January 2025

Five failure modes we address

Failure 01

Fusion gap

OT tags, camera events, permit state and worker location live in different systems. No engine reasons across them in real time.

Failure 02

Cognitive overload

A permit issuer today balances 12–20 checks per hot-work permit. Errors correlate with fatigue, not competence.

Failure 03

Alarm fatigue

Control rooms average 100+ alarms per operator per hour — an order of magnitude above the ISA-18.2 tolerable limit.

Failure 04

Handover blind spots

Verbal handovers lose ≈40% of hazard context. There is no auditable transcript, no comprehension test, no memory.

Failure 05

Post-incident evidence chaos

Assembling an incident evidence bundle takes days. Video, permit revisions, OT traces and radio logs live in ten places.

Our answer

A fusion + reasoning + workflow substrate

Not another EHS database. A real-time compound-risk engine, grounded in a knowledge graph, wrapped in ISA-101 UX.

08 Part II · Product

Design Principles — Halo

Halo is engineered against three simultaneous constraints: life-critical clarity, executive polish, and AI-native fluidity. Seven principles govern every review.

P1Signal over ornament
Every pixel earns its place. Decorative color, gradients and shadows are forbidden.
P2Quiet by default, loud when it matters
Near-monochrome nominal state; color reserved for deviation. No landing-page aesthetics.
P3Progressive intensity
Nominal → warning → critical → catastrophic. Each stage escalates typography, motion, and confirmation friction.
P4Legibility > Density > Scrolling
Never trade legibility for density; never trade density for scrolling. Control-room ergonomics first.
P5Reveal the agent
Every AI action shows what it observed, what it retrieved, what it reasoned, what it called.
P6Every state is a designed state
Empty, loading, error, offline, degraded, muster — all first-class layouts.
P7Keyboard parity + consistent physics
Every mouse action has a keyboard equivalent. Motion follows the same easing across devices.

Compound Risk Intelligence Engine

The Compound Risk Engine is the beating heart of SafetyOS. It expresses hazard patterns as declarative DSL rules, matches them against the live Knowledge Graph plus streaming time-series in under 50 ms, and emits explainable CompoundRisk.v2 events into the agent layer.

%%{init:{'theme':'base','themeVariables':{'primaryColor':'#F7F8F9','primaryBorderColor':'#0B0F14','primaryTextColor':'#0B0F14','lineColor':'#3E4652','fontFamily':'Inter','fontSize':'11px'}}}%% stateDiagram-v2 [*] --> Nominal Nominal --> Watch: single-signal deviation Watch --> Elevated: 2+ signals in graph proximity Elevated --> Critical: pattern DSL match · conf ≥ 0.85 Critical --> Catastrophic: unmitigated · workers exposed Watch --> Nominal: signal returns Elevated --> Watch: single signal clears Critical --> Elevated: HITL suspends permit Catastrophic --> [*]: ER playbook activated
Figure 22.1 Progressive risk escalation. Each transition writes an evidence-linked event.

Signature pattern example

pattern "hot_work_lel_adjacent" v2 {
  when
    permit(type: "hot_work", state: "active", zone: $z)
    adj_zone($z, $adj)
    sensor(type: "LEL", zone: $adj)
      .trend(window: 5m) > 10%LEL
  emit CompoundRisk(
    severity: "critical",
    confidence: min(0.94, sensor.conf),
    evidence: [permit, sensor.series, cv.frames($adj, 5m)],
    recommended_action: "suspend_permit + notify_ER"
  )
}
Figure 22.2 CR-002 — the archetypal compound pattern derived from the Visakhapatnam RCA.

Engine properties

  • Determinism — same graph + same series ⇒ same event.
  • Signed rules — patterns are versioned (SemVer) and cryptographically signed.
  • Shadow mode — every new rule runs in shadow ≥ 30 days before promotion.
  • Explanation payload — evidence set is part of the event, not a post-hoc lookup.
  • Confidence attenuation — degraded sensors reduce, not falsify, confidence.

Ranked benefits

  1. Detects the class of incident that killed workers at Visakhapatnam.
  2. Gives operators ≥ 8 minutes of lead time before the SIF threshold.
  3. Provides insurers & regulators with a reproducible reasoning trace.
